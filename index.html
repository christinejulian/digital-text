TITANIC: LAST NIGHT ABOARD

An Interactive Historical Survival Game

You are a passenger aboard the RMS Titanic on April 14, 1912. The world's largest and most luxurious ship is making its maiden voyage from Southampton, England to New York City. Around you are elegant dining halls, grand staircases, wealthy first-class passengers, hardworking crew members, and families hoping to begin a new life in America. Tonight everything changes. At 11:40 PM, the ship strikes an iceberg in the North Atlantic Ocean. You must make difficult decisions that will determine whether you survive. This game is based on a real historical tragedy and is intended to respectfully educate players about the people, emotions, and events that occurred during the Titanic disaster.

MISSION OBJECTIVE

Your goal is to safely evacuate the ship while helping as many people as possible. Every decision matters. Players will experience:

  • Historical events
  • Moral choices
  • Class differences aboard the ship
  • Time pressure
  • Environmental dangers
  • Human courage and sacrifice

LEVEL 1: THE COLLISION

The room suddenly shakes. Passengers look around in confusion. The engines slow down. Some people laugh and believe it was nothing serious. Crew members begin moving quickly through the hallways. You notice ice scattered across the deck. The air is becoming colder. What do you do?

A. Return to your cabin and gather supplies.
B. Follow the crew to investigate.
C. Help elderly passengers find their way.

LEVEL 2: THE PANIC

Water begins entering the lower levels of the ship. Passengers rush toward the upper decks. Some people refuse to believe the Titanic could sink because they were told it was unsinkable. Lifeboats are being prepared. There are not enough seats for everyone. The darkness around the ship feels endless. The temperature is below freezing. You hear children crying nearby. What do you do?

A. Join a lifeboat line immediately.
B. Help crew members organize passengers.
C. Search for separated family members.

LEVEL 3: THE FINAL HOUR

The front of the ship begins sinking into the ocean. The angle becomes steeper. Furniture slides across the floor. People scream for help. The stars shine brightly above, creating a haunting contrast to the disaster below. You have one final decision.

A. Board the last available lifeboat.
B. Stay behind and assist others.
C. Continue searching for survivors.

EDUCATIONAL MESSAGE

The Titanic disaster reminds us how fragile life can be and how important human compassion becomes during emergencies. The tragedy demonstrated both failures and heroism. It revealed problems with maritime safety regulations, insufficient lifeboat requirements, and overconfidence in technology. Many people showed incredible bravery by helping complete strangers. Others sacrificed their own chances of survival to save families and children. Today, these lessons continue to influence modern ship safety around the world. This story also shows that social class affected survival rates. First-class passengers often had easier access to lifeboats, while third-class passengers faced additional obstacles. Remembering these inequalities helps us better understand history and encourages fairness in modern society. The Titanic is more than a shipwreck. It is a story about humanity. It teaches us that courage, empathy, and cooperation matter even during the darkest moments.

AUTHOR REFLECTION

I chose to transform the story of the Titanic into a historical survival game because interactive media allows people to experience emotions that traditional text alone cannot provide. Players can feel the pressure, uncertainty, and vulnerability experienced by passengers during the disaster. The sensory elements, including darkness, freezing temperatures, panic, and difficult choices, make this narrative ideal for a game adaptation. My goal was to create an educational experience that remains respectful to the real victims and survivors while helping players understand the historical significance of the event. Rather than focusing on entertainment alone, this project encourages empathy and historical awareness.
