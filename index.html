Echoes of the Titanic

April 14, 1912. The RMS Titanic sails across the calm North Atlantic beneath a sky filled with stars. Music echoes through the ship while passengers enjoy what seems like an ordinary evening. You are one of them. Every decision changes your story.

Where do you go?

Walk the Deck Visit the Grand Staircase Explore Third Class
Titanic Fact:
The RMS Titanic was about 882 feet (269 meters) long and was the largest passenger ship in the world when she entered service.

The Boat Deck

The sea is perfectly calm. The cold air bites your face as you admire thousands of stars. Suddenly... You feel a gentle vibration beneath your feet. Passengers exchange confused looks.

Find an Officer Stay Outside
Titanic Fact:
Because the ocean was unusually calm, spotting the iceberg was more difficult than normal.

The Grand Staircase

The orchestra plays while guests laugh. Then glasses rattle. The music briefly stops. A steward quickly walks past without answering questions.

Follow the Steward Ignore the Rumors
Titanic Fact:
The Grand Staircase became one of the most famous parts of the ship because of its elegant woodwork and glass dome.

Third Class

Families gather together, unaware of what has happened. A child asks why the engines have stopped. The corridors slowly become crowded.

Help Families Find Your Cabin
Titanic Fact:
Many third-class passengers were immigrants hoping to begin new lives in North America.

Ending: Survivor

You quickly find an officer. He calmly orders everyone into life jackets. Because you act early, you board one of the first lifeboats. Hours later, the RMS Carpathia rescues you.

Fact:
The Carpathia rescued approximately 710 survivors after receiving Titanic's distress calls.
Play Again

Ending: The Wireless Room

You arrive outside the Marconi Wireless Room. Operators continuously send: CQD... SOS... CQD... SOS... The signals reach nearby ships. You spend the night helping the crew carry messages and supplies. You survive.

Fact:
Titanic was one of the first major disasters where wireless communication played a vital role in saving lives.
Play Again

Ending: Hero

Instead of thinking only about yourself, you help children and families reach lifeboats. Your courage gives others a chance to live. Whether or not history remembers your name, your actions make a difference.

Fact:
Many passengers and crew gave up seats in lifeboats so others could survive.
Play Again

Ending: Lost to the Atlantic

You believe everything will be fine. Minutes pass. The ship begins to tilt. Panic spreads. By the time you reach the lifeboats, they are gone. The freezing Atlantic becomes your final resting place.

Fact:
The water temperature was about 28°F (-2°C), cold enough to cause hypothermia within minutes.
Play Again

Historical Note: The RMS Titanic struck an iceberg late on April 14, 1912, and sank during the early hours of April 15, 1912. More than 1,500 people lost their lives, making it one of the deadliest peacetime maritime disasters in history.
