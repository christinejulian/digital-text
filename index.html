Echoes of the Titanic

April 14, 1912. The RMS Titanic sails across the calm Atlantic beneath a sky filled with brilliant stars. Elegant music echoes through the Grand Staircase while guests enjoy dinner, conversation, and dancing. Crew members confidently walk the polished hallways, believing they are serving aboard the finest ship ever built. Every passenger carries a different dream, yet everyone shares the same destination.

You pause beside the staircase and notice how peaceful everything feels. Outside, the sea is perfectly still, almost too quiet. Suddenly, a faint vibration travels beneath your feet. It lasts only a moment before disappearing. Some passengers briefly stop talking, while others continue laughing as though nothing unusual happened.

You wonder whether it was simply the engines or something more serious.

The Open Deck

Cold air rushes across the railings as you step outside. The moon reflects across the dark Atlantic, making the water appear endless. A sailor quickly walks toward the bridge while another crew member quietly tells passengers to remain calm. Although no official announcement has been made, something about the atmosphere has changed. The confidence that once filled the deck has slowly turned into uncertainty.

Do you trust your instincts?

The Grand Hall

The orchestra resumes playing, but the melody no longer sounds cheerful. A steward politely asks several passengers to wear life jackets. Some immediately listen, while others laugh and insist the Titanic is far too advanced to be in danger. You notice a hallway leading toward your cabin and another leading toward the upper deck.

Ending One — Safe Escape

You choose to trust the crew instead of the rumors around you. After putting on a life jacket, you calmly board one of the first lifeboats. As the boat drifts away, the massive ship slowly tilts into the freezing Atlantic. The lights eventually disappear beneath the water, leaving only silence and scattered stars above. Rescue arrives hours later, and although the memories never fade, your decision allows you to tell the story for the rest of your life.

Ending Two — Too Late

You remain on the deck, convinced that the crew will explain everything soon. The minutes pass slowly as more passengers gather outside. Suddenly, officers begin uncovering lifeboats while worried voices spread across the ship. Panic quickly replaces confidence. By the time you move toward the lifeboats, most have already been lowered into the freezing Atlantic. The bow slips beneath the waves as the lights flicker one final time. The ocean grows silent once again, preserving the story of the Titanic beneath its surface.

Ending Three — Hidden Escape

You hurry back to your cabin to collect your coat and belongings. Along the way, a frightened steward opens a small service corridor normally used only by the crew. He quietly leads a handful of passengers through the passageway to a lifeboat that is preparing to leave. Moments later, you watch from the water as the great ship slowly disappears into the darkness. Your journey continues, but the memory of that night remains with you forever.

Ending Four — The Hero

Instead of leaving immediately, you return to help frightened families find their way to the lifeboats. You guide children through crowded hallways and assist elderly passengers toward safety. Although you miss the first boats, your kindness inspires others to remain calm instead of giving in to fear. Eventually, an officer finds one final space and helps you aboard. As dawn breaks across the Atlantic, you realize that courage is not measured by strength but by choosing to help others when every second matters.
