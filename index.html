Echoes of the Titanic — Dark Historical Edition

April 14, 1912. The RMS Titanic cuts through the Atlantic at 22 knots, ignoring multiple ice warnings. You stand near the Grand Staircase, its gilded ironwork glowing beneath electric lamps. The air smells of perfume, polished oak, and ambition.

A gentle vibration trembles beneath your feet. It lasts only seconds. Most passengers ignore it, unaware that at 11:40 PM, the Titanic has just brushed against an iceberg long enough to open a 300-foot gash along her starboard side. Five compartments flood — one more than she can survive.

Historical Footnotes

• Speed: Titanic traveled at 22 knots despite ice warnings.

• Lifeboats: Only 20 lifeboats — enough for about half aboard.

• Compartments: Titanic could survive 4 flooded compartments; she suffered 5.

• Water Temperature: 28°F — cold enough to kill within minutes.

• Wireless: Titanic was among the first ships to use SOS.

• Breakup: The ship split in two, confirmed in 1985.

• Engineers: The engineering crew stayed at their posts until the end.

• Orchestra: Musicians played until the ship sank.
