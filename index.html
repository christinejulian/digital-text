Echoes of the Titanic

April 14, 1912. The RMS Titanic sails across a calm Atlantic Ocean beneath a sky overflowing with stars. Passengers admire the elegance of the world's newest luxury liner while enjoying music, fine dining, and lively conversation. No one imagines history is only hours away.

You stand beneath the magnificent Grand Staircase. Its polished oak, glittering chandelier, and sweeping staircase make it feel like a floating palace. Nearby, children laugh while wealthy travelers discuss business opportunities awaiting them in New York.

Suddenly... a faint vibration travels through the floor. It is gentle enough that many people barely notice. Others pause, exchanging uncertain looks. The engines slow. Silence spreads through the ship.

Historical Fact:
Titanic struck an iceberg at approximately 11:40 PM on April 14, 1912.

What will you do?

The Boat Deck

Cold air rushes across your face. The Atlantic is strangely calm. Ice floats silently nearby while crew members hurry toward the bridge. Passengers gather along the railings trying to understand what happened.

One officer begins uncovering lifeboats. Many people laugh. Some refuse to believe anything could threaten the world's largest ship.

Historical Fact:
Titanic carried only 20 lifeboats, enough for about 1,178 people although more than 2,200 people were aboard.

The Echo Below

You follow the strange noise into a dark cargo corridor. The sound grows louder—metal groaning, echoing like a voice. Suddenly the lights flicker. A cold wind sweeps through the hallway. You feel watched.

A shadow moves behind you. When you turn, nothing is there. But the whisper remains: "Not all stories end on the surface."

You vanish into the lower decks, becoming a legend whispered by survivors.

Safe Escape

You trust the officers and immediately make your way to the lifeboats. Crew members efficiently lower the boats into the icy Atlantic. Although frightened, you safely leave the ship.

Hours later, the RMS Carpathia rescues the survivors. You spend the rest of your life remembering the courage and sacrifice displayed that night.

Historical Fact:
The RMS Carpathia rescued approximately 705 survivors.
