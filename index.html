Titanic: Race for Survival (Interactive Narrative Game)

Text Object Requirements

Game Title

Titanic: Race for Survival

Type of Narrative

An interactive historical storytelling game based on the real events of the 1912 sinking of the RMS Titanic.

Game Overview

Players experience the final hours aboard the RMS Titanic from the perspective of a passenger. Throughout the game, players must make difficult choices, explore different sections of the ship, help other passengers, and attempt to survive while respecting the historical events that occurred.

Learning Objective

The purpose of this game is to educate players about the Titanic disaster, human decision-making during emergencies, social class differences, and the importance of bravery and cooperation during a crisis.

Story Setting

  • Date: April 14-15, 1912
  • Location: North Atlantic Ocean
  • Ship: RMS Titanic
  • Genre: Historical Interactive Adventure

Player Role

The player becomes a passenger aboard the Titanic during its maiden voyage. Players may choose one of three classes:

  • First Class Passenger
  • Second Class Passenger
  • Third Class Passenger

Game Objectives

  1. Explore the ship.
  2. Collect important information.
  3. Help other passengers.
  4. Make survival decisions.
  5. Reach a lifeboat before time runs out.

Gameplay Mechanics

  • Explore rooms and corridors.
  • Talk to passengers and crew members.
  • Solve small puzzles.
  • Collect historical artifacts.
  • Manage limited time.
  • Make branching decisions.

Player Choices

Examples of decisions players may encounter:

  • Help a lost child or continue toward a lifeboat.
  • Wait for family members or evacuate immediately.
  • Follow crew instructions or search for another route.
  • Share supplies with other passengers.

Sensory Elements

  • Dark environments
  • Cold temperatures
  • Ocean sounds
  • Panic among passengers
  • Emergency alarms
  • Water flooding the ship

Educational Themes

  • Human bravery
  • Leadership
  • Social class differences
  • Teamwork
  • Empathy
  • Historical preservation

Respect Statement

This game is designed to respectfully portray a real historical tragedy. The objective is educational storytelling and historical awareness rather than entertainment through disaster. The experiences are inspired by historical accounts, survivor testimonies, and official investigations.

Target Audience

Students, educators, history enthusiasts, and players interested in interactive historical narratives.
