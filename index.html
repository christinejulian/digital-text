The Forgotten Hallway

Every game begins with a single choice. Sometimes the decision seems small, like opening a door or turning down a hallway. Other times the choice changes everything. This story explores how interactive fiction allows readers to become players. Instead of reading from beginning to end, you guide the experience. Your curiosity becomes part of the narrative, creating a different journey depending on the path you choose.

You arrive at an abandoned school after hearing rumors that strange voices echo through the building every night. The front entrance hangs open, and dusty lockers stretch across the hallway. Every step echoes through the silence. A cold breeze moves through broken windows even though none of them are open. Something feels wrong, yet something invites you deeper inside.

A faded sign points in two directions. One arrow leads toward the old library. The other points to the basement where the lights still flicker. You remember stories about students disappearing years ago, but no one ever discovered what truly happened. The only way to uncover the truth is to keep moving.

As you walk forward, you notice handwritten notes scattered across the floor. Some are warnings. Others are unfinished journal entries written by people who never returned. Every page tells part of the mystery, but none provide a complete answer. The missing pieces can only be found by continuing your exploration.

The atmosphere grows heavier. The silence is interrupted by distant footsteps that stop whenever you stop walking. It feels as if something is watching you from the darkness. You cannot tell whether it wants to help or harm you. The hallway stretches farther than it should, almost like the building itself refuses to let visitors leave.

Interactive stories create suspense because readers participate instead of simply observing. Your decisions shape the experience, making each ending feel personal. Even though every player begins in the same place, their choices produce different outcomes. This creates replay value and encourages exploration. Instead of asking what happens next, the player asks, "What if I choose differently?"

Now the decision is yours.

Ending One: Keeper of Memories

The library shelves are covered in dust, but one book remains perfectly clean. As you open it, the pages begin writing themselves. Every choice you made appears inside the book. You realize the school records every visitor's journey and preserves it forever. You escape safely, but the next time someone enters, they will read your story and decide whether to follow your footsteps.

Ending Two: The Watcher

The basement door locks behind you. The flickering lights reveal dozens of empty chairs facing a single mirror. Your reflection suddenly smiles without your permission. As the lights go dark, you understand the legend was true. The building was never haunted by ghosts—it creates new ones. The next visitor hears footsteps behind them. Those footsteps belong to you.

Ending Three: The Unanswered Mystery

You decide that some mysteries should remain unsolved. Walking back into the daylight feels like waking from a nightmare. Yet when you reach home, your pockets are filled with pages from the abandoned school that you never picked up. Someone—or something—followed you out. The story is over, but the mystery has only begun.
