Echoes of the Titanic

April 10, 1912. The largest passenger ship ever built begins her maiden voyage from Southampton. Passengers from every walk of life fill her magnificent halls. Some seek fortune. Some seek adventure. Some seek a new beginning. No one knows that history will remember this voyage forever. Tonight you are one of those passengers. Every choice you make shapes your fate.

The Upper Deck

The Atlantic is calm. Moonlight dances across the sea while cold air surrounds the ship. Passengers admire the stars. Crew members quietly continue their duties. Hours later... A strange vibration runs beneath your feet. Then silence. Something feels wrong.

The Grand Staircase

Crystal chandeliers sparkle overhead. Music echoes through elegant corridors. Children laugh. Couples dance. Stewards continue serving dinner. Then... Glasses gently shake. The orchestra briefly pauses. Guests exchange puzzled looks before returning to their conversations. What do you do?

Ending One

Saved

A First Officer calmly tells everyone to wear life jackets. You immediately follow instructions. Because you acted quickly, you reach one of the first lifeboats. As your boat lowers into the freezing Atlantic, you watch the great ship slowly disappear beneath the stars. Hours later the RMS Carpathia rescues you. The memories of that night never fade.

The Marconi Wireless Room

Inside the cramped room, wireless operators Jack Phillips and Harold Bride work tirelessly. Their Morse key never stops. CQD. SOS. CQD. SOS. Messages race across the Atlantic. Nearby ships begin answering. You help deliver blankets, life jackets, and messages throughout the ship while hope slowly returns. Eventually rescue arrives. You survive after helping others during the disaster.

Ending Three

Too Late

You decide everything is fine. After all... The Titanic was called unsinkable. Minutes become an hour. The decks tilt. Passengers begin running. Crew members shout for everyone to reach the lifeboats. By the time you arrive... Most have already left. The freezing Atlantic claims the great liner. Your story becomes another lost voice remembered only through history.

Ending Four

Acts of Courage

Instead of searching for a boat immediately, you help frightened families find safety. Children are lifted into lifeboats. Life jackets are shared. Even as the deck tilts, you continue helping strangers. Although you do not survive, your bravery saves many lives. History never records your name, but your actions echo through every survivor's story.

Historical note: The RMS Titanic struck an iceberg late on April 14, 1912, and sank in the early hours of April 15. More than 1,500 people lost their lives, while approximately 710 survived after being rescued by the RMS Carpathia.
